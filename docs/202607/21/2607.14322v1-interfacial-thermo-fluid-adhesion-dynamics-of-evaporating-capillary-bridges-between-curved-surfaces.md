## 摘要
我们探究了连接两个弯曲固体基底的液体毛细液桥的蒸发机制及其相关的粘附动力学。系统地研究了蒸发过程中耦合的热流体物种输运以及毛细粘附力的瞬态演化。开发了一个精确的、完全耦合的瞬态数值框架，其中首先通过水平集方法确定平衡毛细轮廓。然后，通过任意拉格朗日-欧拉（ALE）框架模拟蒸发过程，以精确追踪移动的液-汽界面。全面分析了基底曲率、表面润湿性和固体热导率对蒸发和毛细粘附特性的综合影响。该模拟方法通过与已发表文献中关于毛细轮廓、蒸发速率和毛细力的结果进行对比验证，显示出良好的一致性。我们的结果表明，亲水性和超疏水（SH）液桥的蒸发特性均强烈受控于基底曲率和热导率，且增加这些值有利于提高界面传质速率。固有的非均匀蒸汽通量产生空间变化的蒸发冷却，从而产生表面张力梯度，驱动内部热毛细循环。无量纲尺度分析表明，在整个过程中，马兰戈尼流主导浮力诱导流。此外，增加基底曲率会因液桥曲率减小而降低整体毛细力，而粘附力的时间演化则受到基底曲率和润湿性的强烈影响。

## Abstract
We probe the evaporation mechanism, and the associated adhesion dynamics of liquid capillary bridges connecting two curved, solid substrates. The coupled thermo fluid species transport and the transient evolution of capillary adhesion during evaporation are systematically examined. An accurate, fully coupled transient numerical framework is developed, wherein the equilibrium capillary profiles are first determined from level set method. Next, the evaporation is simulated via Arbitrary Lagrangian Eulerian ALE framework to accurately track the moving liquid vapor interface. The combined influence of substrate curvature, surface wettability, and solid thermal conductivity on evaporation and capillary adhesion character is comprehensively analysed. The simulation methodology is robustly validated against published literature for capillary profiles, evaporation rates, and capillary forces, demonstrating good agreement. Our results reveal that the evaporation characteristics of both hydrophilic and superhydrophobic SH liquid bridges are strongly governed by substrate curvature and thermal conductivity, and increasing values pose favourable condition for augmented interfacial mass transfer rate. The innately non uniform vapour flux generates spatially varying evaporative cooling, producing surface tension gradients that drive internal thermo capillary circulation. A non dimensional scaling analysis shows that Marangoni flow dominates buoyancy induced flow throughout. Also, increasing substrate curvature decreases the overall capillary force, owing to the reduced curvatures of the liquid bridge, while the temporal evolution of the adhesion force is strongly influenced by both substrate curvature and wettability.
