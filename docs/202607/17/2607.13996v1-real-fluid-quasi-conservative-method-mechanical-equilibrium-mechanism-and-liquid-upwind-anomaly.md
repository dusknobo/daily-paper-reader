## 摘要
从连续介质热力学视角重新审视了真实流体有限体积法中的压力振荡问题，并阐明了真实流体准守恒方法的物理对应。该方法通过沿迹线演化等熵内能—压力关系的仿射参数 xi 和 E0 来恢复力学平衡压力，而热力学重投影则将偏离等熵轨迹的偏差转化为内能误差，从而确保方法的热力学一致性和数值稳定性。进而研究了该方法的适用极限，在极端相变情形中发现液体逆风异常。对于涉及液—气相变的黎曼问题，若初始叠加的液体逆风平移速度超过某一阈值，可能出现数值异常。理论分析表明，该异常由相变过程中仿射斜率 xi 的数量级跃变引发，并延迟下游低压单元的压力回升；同时，重投影等效地移除了正向压力增量。结果，此异常单元中反复产生大的重投影内能误差，并陷入延迟低压恢复的循环。分析表明，液体逆风异常是一种启动异常，可通过在初始间断处引入正则化策略加以解决。利用所提出的正则化策略，真实流体准守恒方法在极端热力学流动（如声速相变射流）中具备更高的精度和鲁棒性。

## Abstract
From the perspective of continuum thermodynamics, we revisit the pressure oscillation problem in finite-volume methods for real fluids and clarify the physical counterpart of the Real Fluid Quasi-Conservative (RFQC) method. The RFQC method recovers the mechanical-equilibrium pressure by evolving the affine parameters xi and E0 of the isentropic internal energy-pressure relation along pathlines, while the thermodynamic re-projection converts the deviation from the isentropic trajectory into an internal-energy error, thereby ensuring the thermodynamic consistency and numerical stability of the method. We then investigate the applicability limit of the RFQC method and identify a Liquid-upwind Anomaly (LUA) in extreme phase-change cases. For a Riemann problem involving liquid-vapor phase change, a numerical anomaly may occur if a liquid-upwind translational velocity is initially superimposed and exceeds a certain threshold. Theoretical analysis reveals that this anomaly is initiated by the orders-of-magnitude jump in the affine slope xi during phase change, which subsequently delays the pressure rise in the downstream low-pressure cell. Concurrently, the re-projection equivalently removes the positive pressure increment. As a result, a large re-projection internal-energy error is repeatedly generated in this anomalous cell, and the cell is trapped in a cycle of delayed low-pressure recovery. The analysis indicates that the LUA is a start-up anomaly, which can be resolved by introducing a regularization strategy at the initial discontinuity. With the proposed regularization strategy, the RFQC method is equipped with enhanced accuracy and robustness for extreme thermodynamic flows, such as sonic phase-change jets.
